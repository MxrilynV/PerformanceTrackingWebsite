Abstract

The purpose of this project is to improve upon the existing design of the XPT Self Spotter. The project began with thoroughly examining how the XPT Self Spotter worked and then assessing where improvements could be made. A user interface was necessary in order to improve peak performance and safety on the user's end. The first addition to the XPT would be a device that tracks the amount of weight on the bar. This is accomplished by using a load cell at the machine's base. A rep counter would be the second addition by using a rotary encoder that is installed next to the pulleys that rest on the top of the machine.

1.1 Background

Today, many Americans go to the gym, for a variety of reasons, such as, to stay in shape, be healthy, get stronger, or for competitive reasons. According to Statista, The United States has an estimated 64.19 million gym memberships, the most of any country. Meaning that at least 64.19 million people have gone to a gym to use the exercise machines, weights, and other equipment for their workout. Although each workout is unique, each piece of equipment is used at least once in a gym facility, and among that equipment is a power rack. A power rack is a set of metal bars that surround you while a person is doing their lift. It is commonly used for barbell exercises that include, but are not limited to, squats, bench presses, deadlifts, and overhead presses. Most activities done on the power rack are considered anaerobic strenuous activities. During any form of exercise, your heart beats faster. According to the CDC, for vigorous-intensity physical activity, your target heart rate should be between 77% and 93%, [1] of your maximum heart rate. Powerlifting is a strength training that has become quite popular and has been incorporated into good balance fitness programs [2]. But, no matter how good of an exercise powerlifting is, there is always the risk of injury. Many injuries come from applying too much tension across a specific muscle or tendon. You can also get injured from carrying a weight that you may be too fatigued to lift, or is too heavy for you. For this reason, you may see a spotter, so ensure that the person lifting does not accidentally drop the weight on themselves. When lifting, you are always pushing yourself, to go further so that you can be stronger. You are always pushing your limit. At this point, it is when you have to be the safest. A gym-goer is consumed by their lift and only has one goal, moving the barbell. Losing concentration may stop you from finishing a rep. Constantly depleting your energy bar can lead to fatigue, which causes brain fog. You forget what rep or set you ended up doing. Many repetitions of an exercise are no longer as perfect as the first time. Which is why it is important to have good form when lifting. However, the more fatigue you get, the more brain fog you have, and the harder it is to have good form. All of these factors can cause you to drop the bar on top of yourself, which can severely injure you. You could also end up doing too many reps, leading to a muscle injury. The 2 problems that need to be addressed are dropping the bar and brain fog.

The first aspect to consider is dropping the barbell bar due to fatigue or the bar being too heavy.  Consequently, leading to alternative weight-lifting machines with safety measures such as a smith machine. A smith machine is considered a much safer option than power racks because it does not require the user to balance the bar. It reduces the need for a spotter, by allowing you to rack the barbell into several slots on the frame so that it won’t be dropped. It also allows you to have better form. However, you can only move in a vertical path. The machine is not as versatile as a power rack. Using a power rack, allows you to engage different muscle groups that could not be used in a smith machine. It also allows you to exert more power and have more efficient lifts without being more fatigued [4]. Many use free weight power racks due to their efficiency to reach their fitness goals, whether it be changing body composition, increasing size, and strength, or losing weight. To confront this problem, we will be using the Self Spotting Rack: XPT Pro, which solves one of the problems.

The second issue is mental fatigue. “Mental fatigue can be defined as a psychobiological state caused by prolonged exertion that has the potential to reduce cognitive performance and exercise performance. [5].” Prolonged exertion is overtraining for a sport or physical fitness. Overtraining can be associated with any type of sports or fitness program. As shown in table 3, the symptoms of overtraining syndrome for weightlifting are sympathetic alterations and a lack of mental concentration. Table 3, shows that there is a correlation between exercise and cognitive performance. In order to prevent and put an end to overtraining, our solution is to track the gym-goers performance at the gym while lifting with a phone application that is built with the XPT Pro. The mobile application will be able to track the weight a person is lifting, how many reps they are doing, and check the form of the person by the position of the barbell. These features will solve many issues that are caused by mental fatigue.

2. 1 Design Approach

There are many applications for a variety of reasons that can range from a grocery list to monitoring an appliance. However, there is a limitation to applications used in a gym. All gym apps are all about the same. They offer video training, meal plans, and/or workout tracking. But most of these provided luxuries are pre-set. These applications have videos you can follow, foods that are recommended, and workout trackers that are based on a gym-goer’s manual input to an app. For example, the Nike Training Club App is a great app because it provides users the ability to work out anywhere and anytime with the use of little to no equipment. Another example would be FitnessPoint, which is also a great app. Fitness point allows you to manually enter an exercise you do, gives you a detailed explanation of the lift, and allows you to select the muscle you would like to work on. Other applications would include real-time personal statistics, such as Strava. Strava tracks important stats like speed, pace, elevation, and more statistics based on running, swimming, and cycling. Although these apps are great, their intended targets are those with minimal equipment. Or they don’t track weight lifting and if they do, it is not in real-time.

Upon further research, we were able to discover a similar approach to our design, called Flex. “FLEX measures lift performance and gives powerful real-time feedback through your iPhone, iPad, or Android device [3].” However, it does not ensure the safety of the person using it. We came to the conclusion that weightlifters should be able to safely lift their weights without the worry of injury and have their performance tracked to better understand their progress, change, and have the correct amount of training. We are designing an application and system that fully encompasses the needs and ensures the safety of the user.

Our design phase started with specific objectives that we want to accomplish. We want to calculate the weight, power, distance of the bar travelled, and velocity. All while ensuring the user is safe if the bar becomes too heavy.

Our first step is finding a power rack that is safe for all users and most lifts. This leads us to the XPT Pro. Although it is a safe option, it does not offer performance statistics, which is a key factor in our project. To accommodate this, we look at the mechanical components of the XPT and understand the way it works.

2.2 Rotary Encoder Selection and Calculations

The first input component to implement most of the functions of our design is the pulley system that is being used on the XPT. The pulley neutralizes the force of the bar so that it does not reduce the weight during exercise. There are 4 total counterweight pulleys on each corner of the XPT that are used to eliminate any weight the XPT self-spotting guides may add to the bar. Since the pulleys on the left of the machine always move in unison, one rotary encoder at the further end of the XPT can determine the change in the position of the pulleys.  We can use this to our advantage by incorporating a rotary encoder into the spinning pulley wheel with a shaft at the further end of the XPT to determine the change in the pulley's position.  Due to symmetry, the same can be said about the pulleys on the other sides so one rotary encoder can be used to determine the change in pulley position for that end. Rotary encoders are position and speed sensors we can use to calculate the distance of the bar traveled and the velocity by converting the rotational mechanical displacements into electrical signals and then processing those signals over a given time parameter (seconds). Rotary encoders are our best choice because they are electrical-mechanical devices that do not need manual measurement. It is also very accurate based on how many holes it has. Distance is measured in rotations per minute, and also can be used to measure velocity. Because the encoder is an analog system and the load cell is a digital system. The encoder will require an ADC (Analog to Digital Converter) to convert the electrical signals to digital signals we can read and understand.

        To choose a rotary encoder, first, we pay attention to the type. We have selected a hollow shaft incremental rotary encoder because of its 2 channel system that allows the encoder to determine both clockwise and counterclockwise rotation to determine any change in the XPT’s lateral movement.  The output data they generate provides information to control systems and allows them to determine position, speed, acceleration, deceleration, and travel distance in motion control applications. It consists of a moveable disc with slotted openings to generate. As well as an LED (Light Emitting Diode), a photodetector, to count light pulses as the shaft turns. And lastly, a signal processor that converts the analog waveforms to digital waveforms. The best encoder for our application is an incremental optical encoder that is made with mylar code discs, which would make the encoder robust to shock and vibrations. This is an important consideration because as the weight is constantly being let go, there is always force acting on the power rack, which can lead to inaccurate measurement.

2.3 Load Cell Selection and Calculations

The second and last input component required is the weight and power calculation. However, the XPT pro does not have a weight measuring system, we account for this by applying 2 load cells at the base of the system. The load cell will convert a mechanical force using compression into an electrical signal. The load cell is our best choice because it is also an electrical mechanical system that doesn't need manual measurement. Load cells typically have 4 strain gauges that work together in a Wheatstone bridge.  The Wheatstone is an electrical circuit that balances two legs of a bridge circuit. The force being measured deforms the strain gauge in this type of load cell, and the deformation is measured as a change in the electrical signal.

There are many factors to consider when looking for a load cell for any application. For our project, we have decided to use a compression load cell. The compressed load cell will be installed underneath the base of the XPT and is designed to measure downward compression. Our main reason for this, chosen, was due to placement. After extensive research of the XPT, placing a load cell at the base would give the best reading and accurate results.

The next consideration is knowing the required measurement range, and resolution. The XPT’s max loading weight is 600 lbs and the weight of the XPT is 700 lbs. Meaning, that the load cell must be able to withstand a load of 1300 lb. Bringing our measuring range from 35 (the weight of the barbell) to 600lbs.  As well as a calculated impact force of 8,992.36 lb/f [40kn] whenever the bar is dropped [impact force calc]. As for our resolution, the smallest weight that can be applied for a change in output is 2.5 lbs. We expect changes in increments of at least 0.5 lbs. As for accuracy, it will vary based on our load cell specification as specified in chart 2.

We noticed that the excitation signal from the load cell is in millivolts. This means that an amplifier is needed to bring the signal to volts which will later go to the microcontroller. The amplifier used would be an instrumentation amplifier (IA) because it is able to amplify the input voltage so that it can be used by the ADC of the microcontroller. It is also useful for its ability to common mode rejection. An advantage of using the IA would be that by setting some of the resistors to have equal values, the gain of the IA can be modified just by changing one of the resistors. From the specifications of the load cell input voltage in chart 2 and that most microcontrollers are 3.3, the gain of the IA should be between 137.5 and 275. From these gains, it was calculated, as shown in Appendix C,  that the resistors of the input stage of the IA should have a ratio of 68.25 to 137.

2. 5 Final Product - Implementation

Once we know what parts to get and how they work together to achieve our idea, we connect each component together. Our main goal for the implementation is to take the data we need, and redirect it. Starting from the rotary encoder, we know that our selected incremental optical rotary encoder is a digital system that can be implemented using the hardware. To interface with the encoder, many microcontrollers use a Quadrature Encoder (QEI) API function. The QEI contains a collection of functions for working with the Quadrature Encoder.  There are functions that can help us register a QEI interrupt handler, configure and read the position and velocity captures, and deal with QEI interrupt masking/clearing [6]. We also have a series of interrupts that will work together when we have completed a cycle of data retrieval. When there is a value that has been read into the microcontroller, we want to trigger the interrupt flag and use the Universal Asynchronous Receiver/Transmitter (UART) API. It provides a set of functions for using the Tiva UART modules. Functions are provided to configure and control the UART modules, to send and receive data, and to manage interrupts for the UART modules. It performs serial-to-parallel data conversion on data received from a peripheral device and parallel-to-serial conversion on data transmitted to a peripheral device [6]. UART will be used to interface with WiFi. Wifi does not involve a shared clock signal, so it is asynchronous, which allows us to send data through UART to the WiFi module. By reading the position of the encoder and capturing the velocity, and interfacing with UART to send our data through wifi, we are able to implement most of our intended functions in our project.

The implementation of the load cell requires a little more work, due to the load cell being an analog system. Using an amplifier to the load cell, we are able to receive a strong, stable signal. We will use an ADC to convert the signal into a value we can understand. Over a series of interrupts, the signal from the ADC will be oversampled so that we can  increase resolution and reduce noise. Using the same idea, as our encoder, we will use UART to send data over WiFi.  We are aiming to design a better XPT shoe that can determine the weight of the load-bearing weightlifting bar to two load cell points to reduce production costs. Alternatively, 4 load cells on the corners of the machine will be used to determine the total weight.  

A shoe will be designed and built for the feet of the XPT machine. Its purpose is to house the load cell, microcontrollers, and connecting wires. This is so the load cell can be properly used and that nothing will be in the way of the lifter from performing abnormally. There will be a hole at the back of the shoe where all the wires will come out. It will be hollow in the middle of the shoe in order to encase the load cells and microcontrollers. The shoe will be made up of welded steel beams and then an aluminum ceiling will be added. The aluminum ceiling is so that when the self-spotter applies a force of compression on the load cell the material in between them won’t be too stiff as to not correctly track the weight.

The final aspect of our implementation is our application. The microcontroller will send the data over wifi, which will be received by our Internet of the Things (IoT) Hub. It is a managed service hosted in the cloud that acts as a central message hub for communication between an IoT application and its attached devices. We have also decided to use Azure IoT as it has more versatility and support than other platforms. Meaning the Hub we will be using is Azure IoT Hub, along with Azure IoT central. The IoT Central is a platform where we will centralize all of our microcontroller’s data. It also will allow us to set up and create custom apps.

2. 6 Mobile App

        Our mobile app will include several features that will allow the user to see their performance. When the App opens, the user is greeted with a welcoming page and a “Begin Workout” button. They can add a set by tapping a plus button on the top left corner. This opens a tab that has data coming from the microcontroller, the user will be allowed to see the weight they are lifting. As the user is lifting, a rep is counted every time the bar moves up. On the application, they will see how many reps count they have completed. From there they will have a choice to select to do another set or complete the set. Once a set is completed it will be logged as exercise 1. To make a full workout the user can select all exercises done. Our application design is also specified in Appendix E.
